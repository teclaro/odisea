The essentials in 60 seconds

The whole story in one sentence

A cunning king takes ten years to come home from a war that lasted another ten, while back in his palace 108 suitors devour his estate and pressure his wife to marry one of them.

The king is Odysseus (the Romans called him Ulysses), lord of the island of Ithaca. His weapon is not strength but wit: the Trojan Horse was his idea. His wife is Penelope, every bit the strategist he is, and his son Telemachus was a baby when he left.

Two gods decide his fate: Athena protects him; Poseidon, god of the sea, hunts him. Sailing home when the entire ocean hates you — that is the odyssey.

The trick Nolan inherited from Homer The poem does not start at the beginning: it opens near the end, with Odysseus held captive by the nymph Calypso, and the famous adventures are told later in one great flashback. Film calls it non-linear structure; Homer did it about 2,700 years ago.

Setting sail from Troy0 / 14 stops

After ten years of siege, Troy falls to Odysseus's plan: a giant wooden horse with soldiers hidden inside. With the war over, he sets sail for Ithaca with twelve ships and hundreds of men. It looks like a short trip home. It will not be.

To share at the moviesThe war itself is told in another poem, the Iliad. The Odyssey begins when it is all over: it is the story of the journey home.

First stop, first mistake: they sack the city of the Cicones, allies of Troy. Instead of leaving at once, the men stay to celebrate; the Cicones return with reinforcements and kill several sailors from every ship.

To share at the moviesThe poem states its first lesson right away: arrogance has a price. And Odysseus has not learned his own yet.

A gentle land where people eat the lotus flower. Whoever tastes it forgets home and wants only to stay. Three scouts eat it, and Odysseus has to drag them back to the ships in tears and tie them to the rowing benches.

To share at the moviesThe voyage's true enemy appears here for the first time, and it is not a monster: it is forgetting. The danger of no longer wanting to return.

Trapped in the cave of a one-eyed, man-eating giant, Odysseus plots the perfect escape: he gets him drunk, tells him his name is “Nobody”, blinds him with a burning stake, and slips out with his men clinging beneath the sheep of the flock.

Safely at sea, vanity betrays him: he shouts his real name. Polyphemus, son of Poseidon, begs his father to make Odysseus suffer the whole sea before reaching home. The curse that shapes the entire voyage is born here.

The eye is watching you… touch it if you dare.

To share at the moviesWhen the other Cyclopes rush to the screams and ask who is attacking him, Polyphemus answers: “Nobody!”. So they leave. The best joke in ancient literature.

The lord of the winds hands them a safe-conduct: a leather bag with every adverse wind locked inside. They sail for nine days and come close enough to see the watch-fires of Ithaca on the horizon.

While Odysseus sleeps, the crew opens the bag, convinced it hides gold. The winds escape, the storm drags them back to where they started, and Aeolus refuses to help a man so clearly cursed by the gods.

To share at the moviesThey were so close they could see the smoke of their own homes. It is the cruelest moment in the poem: losing everything to distrust and greed.

A perfect bay that turns out to be a trap: the Laestrygonians are man-eating giants who bombard the fleet with boulders from the cliffs and spear the men. Eleven of the twelve ships sink with everyone aboard.

Only Odysseus's ship escapes — he had anchored it outside the bay as a precaution. Of hundreds of men, a single crew remains.

To share at the moviesIt is the greatest massacre of the voyage and it takes just a few lines of the poem. From here on, the story turns intimate: one ship, a handful of men.

On the island of Aeaea, the sorceress Circe invites the scouts to a feast and turns them into pigs. Odysseus goes to rescue them armed with a magical herb, moly, given to him by the god Hermes, which makes him immune to her spell.

Defeated, Circe restores the men and becomes an ally: she hosts them for a whole year and reveals the next step, the darkest of all. To learn the way home, Odysseus must consult the seer Tiresias. The problem: Tiresias is dead.

To share at the moviesCirce is no simple villain: she goes from threat to guide. Without her instructions, Odysseus would never have survived what comes next.

Odysseus sails to the edge of the world and summons the spirits. Tiresias delivers the prophecy that orders the rest of the voyage: he will reach home, alone and on a stranger's ship, if no one touches the sacred cattle of the Sun.

He also speaks with his mother, Anticlea, and learns she died of grief waiting for him. And with Achilles, the great hero of Troy, who confesses he would rather be the lowest living servant than king of all the dead.

To share at the moviesAchilles's line is a brutal reversal: the warrior who chose glory and an early death regrets it. The whole poem argues the opposite: life, home, the return.

Their song promises all the knowledge in the world, and whoever hears it steers into the rocks and dies. Following Circe's advice, Odysseus seals his men's ears with wax and orders them to tie him to the mast — and not release him no matter how much he begs.

And so he becomes the only mortal to hear the Sirens and live to tell it.

To share at the moviesTrue to himself: Odysseus does not give up the temptation — he engineers it. He wants to hear everything, but designs the system that keeps him from jumping.

A strait with death on both sides: Scylla, a six-headed monster living in the cliff, and Charybdis, a whirlpool that swallows ships whole. There is no safe route; you only choose how to lose.

Odysseus sails close to Scylla and sacrifices six men, one per head, rather than risk the whole ship in the whirlpool. It is the coldest decision of the voyage — and the most like a captain's.

To share at the moviesThis is where the expression “between Scylla and Charybdis” comes from: choosing between two evils. The Greek version of “between a rock and a hard place”.

Stranded without wind on the island of the god Helios, with Tiresias's warning still ringing: do not touch the sacred cattle. Hunger bites for weeks and, while Odysseus sleeps, his men slaughter the finest cows.

The punishment is total: the moment they sail, Zeus shatters the ship with a thunderbolt. Everyone drowns. Everyone but one, clinging to the wreckage of the mast.

To share at the moviesThe prophecy comes true with mathematical precision: he will arrive alone. From here on, the Odyssey stops being the story of a crew and becomes the story of a survivor.

The castaway washes up on Ogygia, the island of the nymph Calypso, who falls in love with him and keeps him for seven years. She offers the ultimate deal: stay with me and be immortal, young forever.

Odysseus says no. He would rather grow old and die beside Penelope than live forever far from home. Athena intercedes with Zeus, Hermes delivers the order to release him, and Odysseus builds a raft and returns to the sea that hates him.

To share at the moviesThis is where the poem actually begins: Book Five opens with Odysseus weeping by the shore on this island. Everything before it is told later, in flashback.

Poseidon smashes the raft, but Odysseus swims to the island of the Phaeacians, a legendary nation of sailors. Princess Nausicaa finds him on the beach, naked and broken, and brings him to the court of her father, King Alcinous.

There, an anonymous guest bursts into tears when a poet sings of Troy. Then he reveals his name and tells every adventure you have just read. Moved, the Phaeacians heap him with gifts and carry him home to Ithaca, asleep, aboard one of their ships.

To share at the moviesWe know the famous adventures (the Cyclops, the Sirens, Circe) from Odysseus's own mouth — a cunning narrator telling his own legend. How much did he embellish? Homer leaves the question open.

Twenty years after leaving, Odysseus sets foot on his island. Athena disguises him as a beggar so he can measure who stayed loyal. He secretly joins forces with Telemachus and with Eumaeus, his faithful swineherd. His old dog, Argos, knows him instantly and dies in peace. His nurse Eurycleia recognizes him by a childhood scar.

Penelope, unaware of who the beggar is, announces the trial of the bow: she will marry whoever can string Odysseus's bow and shoot an arrow through twelve axes in a row. The suitors fail one by one. The beggar strings it like a bard tuning a lyre, shoots, hits — and with Telemachus he bars the doors of the hall. The reckoning is complete.

One test remains, the hardest: Penelope, cautious after twenty years, orders their bed moved to see how he reacts. Odysseus protests: impossible — he carved it himself around a living olive tree rooted in the earth. Only the two of them knew that secret. Only then does Penelope embrace him. Athena seals the peace on the island.

To share at the moviesPenelope fought her own war: she promised to choose a husband once she finished weaving a shroud, and every night she unwove the day's work. She fooled 108 men for years. In cunning, this marriage is an even match.
